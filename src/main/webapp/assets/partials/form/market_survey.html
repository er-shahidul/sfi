SFI Market Survey

The reasons for certifying to the SFI Standards are as diverse as the more than 1,000 organizations that participate, and as diverse as the forests of North America. In order to ensure that your participation renders the value that you need and expect from SFI, it is important for us to understand your reasons for participating. It’s just as important that SFI provides value to you by helping to ensure meaningful linkage between the sustainability attributes and conservation values of managed forests, and the sustainability requirements and expectations of buyers, customers, conservation stakeholders, and the general public. In this way, you can better utilize this information to convey the inherent values and attributes of your certified products.

As an organization certified to one or more of the SFI standards, we would appreciate your perspective on the value of SFI certification and your involvement in the SFI program. While completing this market survey is optional, you are strongly encouraged to respond and help SFI render value back to you as a Program Participant. SFI will use the results to generate insights and identify opportunities to better serve all SFI organizations certified to the SFI standards.

Please note that individual responses shall remain strictly confidential, and may only be used in aggregate form for marketing and communications purposes.

We know demand for certified products in the wood, print, paper and packaging sectors is on the rise. Based on a 2014 study conducted by the research firm GfK, almost 70% of 1,000 U.S. consumers said they would prefer companies that source wood-based products responsibly to use a forest certification label to inform consumers. We also know from a survey by The Natural Marketing Institute (NMI) that, of more than 20,000 U.S. consumers, 24% recognized and understood the SFI logo, compared to 15% for FSC.

Last year SFI sent a market survey to SFI Primary Producers (SFI Program Participants who source fiber directly from the forest), and over 90% responded. The resulting information was used to produce an SFI Market Relevancy Factsheet, which we hope you may find useful in promoting the value of SFI. This year SFI is expanding the survey to SFI Secondary Producers as well (SFI Program Participants such as printers, paperboard manufacturers, and wood brokers).

Some Examples of Linkage Through the Supply Chain

The SFI community is incredibly diverse and the value-added actions of Program Participants partners, conservationists, and local SFI Implementation Committees provide opportunities to capture and convey value in your own organization. This is a community that includes Fortune 500 companies, small and mid-sized forestry operations, government agencies, manufacturers, academic institutions, conservation organizations, Aboriginal and Tribal groups, and even community groups like Boy Scouts of America. Collectively, their achievements and engagement in SFI reach far beyond the bounds of the standard, and render values that support economies and quality of life throughout North America.

By responding to this part of the survey, SFI can help create links between you and the conservation and community stories that your SFI participation helps support.
